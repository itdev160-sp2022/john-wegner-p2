Dog Breed Information

The dog is one of the most popular pets in the world. It's a domesticated descendant of the wolf and is characterized by an upturning tail. It's derived from an ancient, extinct wolf. Therefore, it's closest living relative is the modern wolf. It was the first species to be domesticated, which was by hunter-gathers over 15,000 years ago. This was long before agriculture. Because of their long association with humans, they gained the ability to thrive on a starch-rich diet that'd be inadequate for other canids. If you'd like to learn more information about dogs, please visit the official Wikipedia page on dogs.

Dog Breeds

Airedale Terrier

  1. Height: 22-24 inches (Male), 22-23 inches (Female)
  2. Weight:40-50 pounds (Male), 35-40 pounds (Female)
  3. Coat: Broken
  4. Color: Black saddle with tan ears, legs, and head; dark grizzle saddle (black mixed with gray and white)
  5. Litter Size: 9 pups
  6. Life Span: 7-14 years
  7. American Kennel Club Link: Airedale Terrier

Irish Wolfhound

  1. Height: 31-34 inches (Male), 28-34 inches (Female)
  2. Weight: 120 pounds (Male), 89 pounds (Female)
  3. Coat: Rough and hard on the head, body and legs; beard and hair over eyes particularly wiry
  4. Color: Black, brindle, fawn, grey, red, pure white, or any color seen in the Deerhound
  5. Litter Size: 4-10 pups
  6. Life Span: 6-10 years
  7. American Kennel Club Link: Irish Wolfhound
